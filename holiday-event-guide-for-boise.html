Holiday Event Guide for Boise

February 05, 2024
blog

BOISE, Idaho (CBS2) — The holiday season is almost upon us, and many fun holiday-related events are happening in Boise this year:

Simply Cats' Halloween Trick or Treat Open House

2833 S. Victory View Way

Oct. 27 from noon to 3 p.m.

More information: There will be raffles, candy, and kitties! The raffle winners will be drawn the following week. Bring donation items for the kitties and earn four raffle entries towards any of the four raffle prizes. All proceeds will help Simply Cats fundraise money to help cats and kittens in need.

Taste of Downtown Boise

Oct. 27 to Nov. 5

More information: You are invited to experience the local ingredients and purveyors that make our Downtown Boise food and beverage scene fresh and exciting. Enjoy a $12 special, social hour, prix-fixe menu and experience at nearly 50 fantastic downtown establishments over these 10 days.

Boo at the Zoo, presented by Delta Dental of Idaho

Zoo Boise, 355 Julia Davis Dr.

Oct. 28 and 29 from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.

More information: Trick-or-treat candy stations, costume contests for all ages, vendors, face painting, special Halloween-themed treats for the zoo animals, and more await the entire family over Halloween weekend!

Broadway in Boise Presents My Fair Lady

Morrison Center for the Performing Arts, 2201 W. Cesar Chavez Ln.

Nov. 8 from 7:30 p.m. to 10 p.m.

More information: From Lincoln Center Theater, which brought you The King & I and South Pacific, comes "a sumptuous new production of the most perfect musical of all time" (Entertainment Weekly), Lerner & Loewe's MY FAIR LADY.

Boise Phil presents Beethoven 5 (Matinee)

Morrison Center for the Performing Arts, 2201 W. Cesar Chavez Ln.

Nov. 11 from 1 to 3:30 p.m.

More information: Beethoven's Fifth Symphony stands apart as one of the most recognizable works ever written, and a live performance of the Fifth never fails to inspire. From the fateful opening hammer strokes to its rapturous finale, the music comes alive with an intensity impossible to ignore.

NUTCRACKER! Magical Christmas Ballet

Egyptian Theatre, 700 W Main St.

Nov. 14 from 7 to 9 p.m.

More information: Gather your loved ones for the warm-glow nostalgia of America's favorite Christmas celebration. Experience the extraordinary international cast and Ukrainian Principal Artists performing at the peak of classical European Ballet.

Guardian Ball XII: A Taste of New Orleans – Masquerade Ball

JUMP (Jack's Urban Meeting Place), 1000 Myrtle St.

Nov. 17 from 5 to 11 p.m.

More information: Join for the Taste of New Orleans Masquerade. There will be a live auction, dinner, music, games and prizes, and an opportunity to meet our Warriors. During the evening's program, we will honor our Warrior of the Year, Volunteer of the Year, and Community Mentor of the Year.

Breakfast with Santa at Zoo Boise

Zoo Boise, 355 Julia Davis Dr.

Dec 9 and 16 from 8:30 to 10 a.m.

More information: Guests will be treated to a buffet breakfast, personal time with Santa Claus, kids craft time, animal experiences, and photos with Santa (if purchased in advance). Then, explore the Zoo for the rest of the day.
